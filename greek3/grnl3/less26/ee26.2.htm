Lesson 26.2: ATHEMATIC AORIST OF MI VERBS: MIDDLE VOICE

 

Remember that athematic verbs such as τίθημι, δίδωμι, ἵημι[1], and ἵστημι alternate long and short forms of the stem vowel in the PRESENT SYSTEM of the ACTIVE VOICE.  In the AORIST ἵστημι was included in the chart of the active voice only for contrast, because neither of its two aorists resemble those of the other three verbs: one is a root aorist (athematic but with a long vowel), the other is a first aorist.  ἵστημι cannot be included here either, because its root aorist, like most root aorists, exists only in the active voice.  In this chart, therefore, I include a deponent short vowel athematic aorist of another verb:  ἐπριάμην.
