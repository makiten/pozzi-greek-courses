22.2: Introduction to the Optative

 

The Optative is found only sparsely in koinê Greek, yet in order to interpret its occurrences it is important to understand the basic functions of this mood.  This page presents the functions  in a general way;  22.3 gives clues to the recognition of Optative forms in the different tenses and voices, and 22.4 has examples of the use of Optatives in koinê Greek.

Notes

1) As you surely remember, the aorist denotes time only in the Indicative (the aorist infinitive and participle may refer to the time of the main verb, e.g. in indirect speech).  Otherwise it indicates only punctual aspect.  So, just as in the Subjunctive, present and aorist forms of the Optative will warrant the same translation.
