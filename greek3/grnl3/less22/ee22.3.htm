22.3  Conjugation of the Optative

Again it will be useful to compare the formula for the construction of a Subjunctive with that of an Optative.  This is very general, it applies to stems in different tenses and voices.  On the other hand, these formulas do not take into consideration changes due to the encounter of sounds.  As an illustration, the first person (active voice) of the present and three types of aorists are given below.

Contrast between Subjunctive and Optative forms:

PRESENT SUBJUNCTIVE       stem  +  long thematic vowel + primary endings1

                                                       πείθ-ω, πείθ-ῃς, πείθ-ῃ, πείθ-ωμεν, πείθ-ητε, πείθ-ωσι

PRESENT OPTATIVE               stem  +  ι or ιη  (MARK OF THE OPTATIVE) + secondary endings2

                                        πείθ-οι-μι, πείθ-οις, πείθ-οι, πείθ-οιμεν, πείθ-οιτε, πείθ-οιεν

Note that:

a)      When the Optative has a thematic or connecting vowel, it is ο in all the persons;

b)  The optative, (even the optative of  -ω  verbs)  takes the ending  -μι  in the 1st sg active.
