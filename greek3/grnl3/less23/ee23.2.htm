23.2  Syntax of Comparatives and Superlatives

Construction of the point of comparison   

A comparison in English has the structure:  X is -er than Y.  In Greek this second element of the comparison may be expressed in a similar way, using the conjunction  ἤ and putting the point of comparison  in the same case as the first element compared. Note that ἤ  =  than after a comparative should not be confused with  ἤ  =  or.

Another frequent construction of the point of comparison puts it in the genitive without any connector.

 

1)  ἤ + POINT OF COMPARISON IN THE SAME CASE:

  

οἱ Ἀθηναῖοι ἐμπειρότεροι ἦσαν τῆς ναυτικῆς τέχνης ἢ οἱ Λακειδαιμόνιοι.

                  S                           PN                        V            gen w/ ἔμπειρος                          point of comparison

The Athenians were more experienced than the Spartans in navigation.

 

οὐδέποτε ἑωράκαμεν ἵππους ταχίονας ἢ τούτους.

          adv                       V                                DO                        point of comparison

We have never seen horses faster than these.

 

2) POINT OF COMPARISON IN THE GENITIVE

 

οἱ Ἀθηναῖοι ἐμπειρότεροι ἦσαν τῆς ναυτικῆς τέχνης  τῶν Λακειδαιμονίων

                  S                           PN                        V            gen w/ ἔμπειρος                       point of comparison

The Athenians were more experienced than the Spartans in navigation.

οὐδέποτε ἑωράκαμεν ἵππους ταχίονας  τούτων.

          adv                       V                                DO                        point of comparison

 

Construction of the complement of the superlative

 

A relative superlative is often accompanied in English by something like "of all..."  In Greek too, a partitive genitive may give the complement of a superlative:

 

Ἀχιλλεὺς ἐβούλετο καλεῖσθαι      ἄριστος     πάντων. 

            S                         V            infinitive             PN  through infin       compl of the superlative

 

Achilles wished to be called the bravest of all men.

 

 Ἀφροδίτη ἦν καλλίστη τῶν ἐν Ὀλύμπῳ θεῶν.

            S               V              PN                   compl of the superlative

 

Aphrodite was the most beautiful of the goddesses in Olympus.
