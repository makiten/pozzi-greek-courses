21.3 Result clauses

 

Result clauses are also called consecutive clauses because they represent the consequence or effect of the main clause.  Typically there is an element in the main clause that indicates that either the action of the verb or its circumstances, or another concomitant element, happen or have happened to such an extent, so intensely, in such a way... that the result clause may ensue or ensues. 

 

Example in English[1]:    This ad was so outrageous as to create an adverse reaction.  (possible consequence)

                                                He was so tired that he fell asleep in class. (actual consequence)

 

A result clause in Greek is introduced by  ὥστε (sometimes by   ὡς) and has two possible structures:[2]

ὥστε  + Infinitive, if the result is one that the main clause tends to produce (English may say "so ... as."  Negation:  μή

ὥστε  + Indicative or any other form of statement, if an actual result is indicated (less common in NT Greek than in Classical Greek).  In English usually "so... that."  Negation:  οὐ

[1]  Notice the difference from an English clause of purpose.  If we say in English "She hurries in order to arrive in time" or "She hurries to arrive in time" confusion will not occur. But if the subjects of the main clause and the purpose clause are different, we tend to say "She hurries so that no one will accuse her of being always late." Do we have a purpose clause that sounds like a result clause?  Not really: note that the "so" in "so that" does not reinforce an adjective or an adverb, as it does in the main clause that will be followed by a result clause, where we will find something like, "She started out so early ... that all were asleep."

[2]  Sometimes ὥστε is found at the beginning of a sentence, to mark a strong conclusion.  It may be translated as and so, therefore (equivalent to οὖν).  In this use there are two main clauses; there is no emphasis in the first clause on the extent, intensity, or manner of the action.  

Example:

καὶ ἔλεγεν αὐτοῖς·  Τὸ σάβαττον διὰ τὸν ἄνθρωπον ἐγένετο καὶ οὐχ ὁ ἄνθρωπος διὰ τὸ σάββατον·  ὥστε κύριός ἐστιν ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου καὶ τοῦ σαββάτου.

He said to them, The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the Sabbath. Therefore the Son of Man is lord even of the Sabbath.  (Mark 2:27-28)
