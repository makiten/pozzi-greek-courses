21.2:  Principal parts of irregular verbs

 

This page groups together a few irregular verbs, common both in ancient and in Biblical Greek.  Seven classes of verbs are mentioned here, as usual, a simplification of traditional theory.  Understanding the contrasts between the apparently unrelated stems of irregular verbs will make it easier to recognize and remember them.

 

1)  This is the class of regular, predictable verbs.  In them the present stem = the verb stem.   The "verb stem" generates all the other stems of the conjugation, by means of the addition of tense markers and the appropriate endings. 

 

Ex.   παύω, παύσω, ἔπαυσα, πέπαυκα, πέπαυμαι, ἐπαύθην = to stop

Present stem:  παυ-.  Verb stem: παυ-.  Tense markers and endings: For the future indicative we add  σ; for the aorist, augment and  -σα;  for the active perfect, reduplication and  -κα ; for the middle-passive perfect, reduplication and  -μαι , etc.  See 20.1 to review tenses and voices derived from each stem.

 

In all the remaining classes of  verbs that I will list here, the present stem consists of the verb stem + additional elements.  If we are aware of this, we will not include this addition when we form other tenses.  In classes 2-5 there is an element (two in #3) added to the present stem which do not occur elsewhere; class 6 shows a contrast between the present stem and a strong stem comparable  to "swim / swam" in English; class 7 presents unrelated stems, comparable  to English "go/went."

 

2) Ex.  κόπ-τ-ω,  κόψω, ἔκοψα, κέκοφα, κέκομμαι = to cut, beat.

Present stem:  κοπ-τ.  Verb stem:  κοπ-.  So, despite appearances, this is a labial verb.

 

3) Ex. μα-ν-θ-άν-ω,  μαθήσομαι, ἔμαθον, μεμάθηκα  = to learn (with an η expansion in the future and in the perfect)Recognizing this you can predict rather successfully the principal parts of such verbs as  λα-ν-θ-άν-ω,   τυ-γ-χ-άν-ω .  Consider the second aorists  ἔμαθον, ἔλαθον, ἔτυχον.

 

4)  In a number of verbs  ϳ (“yod,” a semiconsonant that sounded like “y” in English “yes”) was added in the present, with two  main phonetic results.

 

·         With palatal stems, the combination resulted in  -σσω  (Attic   -ττω)  Comparison with a noun or another related lexical item helps us discover the real verb stem.

 

Ex. πράσσ-ω  (Attic  πράττ-ω ),  πράξω, ἔπραξα, πέπραγα, πέπραγμαι, ἐπράχθην = to accomplish, do.  Cf   πρᾶγμα, πράγματος, τό.  The verb stem is  πραγ-. When ϳ was added,  γϳ >  σσω1

 

·         With λ, the addition of  ϳ  led to the duplication of the λ.

 

Ex. ἀγγέλλ-ω, ἀγγελῶ, ἤγγειλα, ἤγγελκα, ἤγγελμαι, ἠγγέλθην = to announce. Cf   ἄγγελος.

The verb stem is  ἀγγελ-, with one λ. When ϳ was added, λϳ > λλ, but in the other tenses there is only one λ.

 

5) Especially in verbs denoting a gradual or progressive action, the suffix  -σκ- appears in the present stem but not in the verb stem:

Ex.  εὑρί-σκ-ω, εὑρήσω, εὗρον  or   ηὗρον, εὕρηκα, εὕρημαι, εὑρέθην = to find

 

6) Sometimes the present stem and the so-called “second” or “strong” aorist stem are contrasted. 

 Ex. φεύγ-ω /  ἔ-φυγ-ον,  μανθάν-ω /  ἔ-μαθ-ον.  The second aorist tends to present the pure verbal stem, before additions or modifications.

 

And, finally...

 

7) In the most irregular or unpredictable verbs, stems of different origins are associated to become parts of a common meaning.  There is no resource other than remembering them.

 

Ex.  φέρω, οἴσω, ἤνεγκον or  ἤνεγκα, ἐνήνοχα, ἐνήνεγμαι, ἠνέχθην = to carry, bear, bring

The stems are:  φερ-,  οἰσ-,  ἐνεκ-, ἐνεγκ-

You will find  in Lesson 22 a short list of the irregular verbs you are expected to remember.
