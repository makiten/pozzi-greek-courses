27.3:  Temporal  clauses

 

type

conjunction and structure

examples

 

Referring to a past action or event with respect to the main verb

                or

one simultaneous with the man verb

INDICATIVE

ἐπεί[1] (= after, when),

ἐπειδή[2] (= after, when),

ἐξ οὗ (= since //the time when//)

ὅτε (= when), ὁπότε (= when)

ἕως (= while),  ἐν ᾧ   (= while)

 

Referring to the future

 

as a fact or event

 

INDICATIVE

ἕως, μέχρι  = until

 

INFINITIVE

πρίν, πρότερον ἤ = before (the main clause is positive or negative)

 

ἔμειναν ἕως ἀφίκοντο οἱ στρατηγοί

They waited until the generals arrived X. H. 1.1.29

 

 ποιήσω (or  οὐ ποιήσω ) τοῦτο πρίν σε κελεῦσαι

I will /will not/  do that before you command //it//

Referring to the future

 

as  a possibility

 

SUBJUNCTIVE  +  ἄν   (with future or equivalent in the main clause)

ὅταν, ἐπειδάν = when, after (in the future)

ἕως ἄν  + SUBJUNCTIVE  = until

πρίν ἄν   + SUBJUNCTIVE  = until

 (only if the main clause is negative)

 

 

(ἐπὰν δὲ εὕρητε) [ἀπαγγείλατέ μοι.]  Mt 2:8

When you (will) find him, let me know.

 

 ἐπειδὰν πάντα ἀκούσατε, κρίνατε When you (will) have heard everything, decide D. 4.14

 

  οὐ ποιήσω  τοῦτο πρὶν ἂν κελεύσῃς

I won’t do that until you command //it//.

 Generalizing

(in the present)

 

SUBJUNCTIVE  +  ἄν   (with present in the main clause)

 

 (ὅταν ἀσθηνῇς,) [τότε δυνατός εἶ.]

 When(ever) you (sg) are weak, then you are powerful.

 

 ... φωνή τις, ἥ, ὅταν γένηται, ἀεὶ ἀποτρέπει με ... a kind of voice which, whenever it comes, always deters me P. A. 31d

(in the past)

 

not yet...

  

NOTE:

After an affirmative clause πρίν usually takes the infinitive and means before.

The subjunctive  +  ἄν is never used with πρίν unless the principal clause is negative. Then it is construed like

ἕως and other words for until.

[1]  Also = since (introducing a causal clause)
