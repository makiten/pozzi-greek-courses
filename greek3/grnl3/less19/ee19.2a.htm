19.2a Forms of the Active Perfect

 

To form a regular (first) active perfect indicative, such as  πε-πόρευ-κα, combine the following elements:

 

reduplication + present stem+ the tense suffix - κα + personal endings

 

Reduplication of the perfect:

1) For a verb beginning with a consonantal sound, reduplication is a prefix combining the first consonant of the present verbal stem, in this case           π, with  ε = πε.  

                                 ... but

·        If the present stem begins with an aspirate ( θ, φ, χ ) reduplication is formed with the corresponding unvoiced stop, ( τ, π, κ , in the same order):  τέθυκα, πεφίληκα, κέχρημαι.

·        For a verb beginning with  κλ, κρ, τρ, δρ, or the like (i.e. the combination of a stop and a liquid), use only the first consonant: 

Present   δράω           Perfect δέδρακα

·        For a verb beginning with two consonants, a double consonant such as  ζ, ξ, ψ, or the consonant  ῥ, use  ε as reduplication:

Present   ζητέω        Perfect  ἐζήτηκα

2) For a verb beginning with a vowel, reduplication looks like augment (no confusion will occur, if we look at the primary endings of the perfect)

                       Present  ἄγω, ἄξω, ἤγαγον, ἦχα

Note that α  becomes  η  in the perfect, just as it does in the second aorist.  Click here to see how an initial vowel lengthens in augment.  It lengthens the same way in reduplication.

 

When in the pluperfect it is necessary to add augment to reduplication, again the same rules apply.  Consider these pluperfects, where, since the initial vowel of the stem has already been lengthened for the purpose of reduplication, no further lengthening can occur:

ὀνομάζω, ὀνομάσω, ὠνόμασα, ὠνόμακα (plupf  ὠνoμάκη), ὠνομάσθην                                            

ἄγω, ἄξω, ἤγαγον, ἦχα (plupf  ἦχη), ἤχθην

3) For an irregular verb, only the citation will give you the clue: 

ἀκούω has  ἀκήκοα, ἔρχομαι has  ἐλήλυθα, λαμβάνω has  εἴληφα, and so on.

Personal endings: apply them directly after the tense suffix. The perfect has no thematic vowel. These are primary endings, but different from those used in the present indicative
