BIBLIOGRAPHY

On dictionaries

You know enough Greek now to be able to consult a dictionary with a minimum of frustration.  In the second semester I continue listing and linking new vocabulary to a total list.  As the number of words used in the course increases, and the inevitable initial gaps in my listings become apparent, you may resort to one of these solutions: looking up a dictionary online, or buying a small dictionary.  I list these resources separately for ancient and Biblical Greek, starting with the cheapest.  The links take you to Amazon or Barnes and Noble,  but you may also search for used copies elsewhere.

Ancient Greek Dictionaries:

The Pocket Oxford Classical Greek Dictionary (Paperback)    (around $11)
by James Morwood (Editor), John Taylor (Editor)

A Lexicon: Abridged from Liddell and Scott's Greek-English Lexicon

Sufficient for an undergraduate student of ancient Greek ($ 35).  Yet for only a few extra dollars the next size is far more useful:

An Intermediate Greek-English Lexicon   ($ 43)

The large Liddell and Scott ($135) is a tool for scholars and advanced students.

Online Liddell-Scott:

Go to Perseus.  You will need to follow the instructions in the Display, and then click on Tools.  One of the options will be Greek Dictionary entry. In the window that opens with the Intermediate L-S entry you will find a link to the larger L-S and to the Intermediate L-S.  An extraordinary tool, though not terribly easy to use...

Koinê Greek Dictionaries:

A Concise Dictionary of New Testament Greek  by Warren C. Trenchard; Paperback 
new: $17.99 -- Used & new from: $9.00  

Two larger editions, that someone who plans to do scholarly research in koinê  may consider, are:

A Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament and Other Early Christian Literature,  ($ 44).  This edition includes only New Testament sources, to the exclusion of the Apostolic Fathers and other early Christian literature:

A Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament and Other Early Christian Literature (Walter Bauer, William Arndt, Frederick W. Danker, ed.,  University of Chicago Press 2000.  ($ 68).  More comprehensive than the above.
