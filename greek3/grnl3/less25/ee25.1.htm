25.1: Athematic verbs: ἵημι

Now that you are familiar with the conjugation of τίθημι, you will be able to recognize the forms of ἵημι , even though this is perhaps the most challenging of the athematic verbs we are learning.

On this page we study the Present System, in the Indicative, Subjunctive, and Optative. Other tenses will be explained later and the Imperative, Infinitive, and Participle will be added in Lesson 27, for all the athematic verbs studied up to that point.

In the active and middle present indicative, ἵημι will have most of the time the same endings as in τίθημι, since both verbs have a stem ending in ε/η.  

Citation:  ἵημι, ἥσω, [ἧκα], εἷκα, εἷμαι, εἵθην

The rough breathing of ἵημι results from an early initial “yod,” represented as ϳ, a semiconsonant that disappeared in Greek altogether. Its pronunciation may have been similar to English “y” in “yes.” So the original stem: ϳ ε / ϳ η became ἑ/ἡ, and the reduplication followed suit, producing ἱ-ἑ/ἡ or rather, with only one rough breathing, ἱ-ε/η.

The features of Present system of ἵημι similar to those of τίθημι, are as follows:

 

  • The Present system is marked by reduplication, formed with the first consonant (reduced in ἵημι to a breathing) + ι;
  • It has a special set of personal endings, some of which, however, are the same as those of ω verbs.
  • Long and short forms of the stem vowel alternate, with a long in the active singular of the Indicative, contrasted with a short vowel in the active plural and all middle/passive forms. Some persons of the Imperfect singular of both verbs are irregular. They are explained in several ways: the simplest is that a thematic vowel has “intruded” in these and other forms that we will study later, and the long sound ει (instead of η) results from ensuing contraction. Just as in τίθημι this occurs in the 2nd and 3rd persons singular of the Active Imperfect.
  • In the Subjunctive the stem vowel becomes short before another vowel and then contracts with it;
  • In the Optative the stem vowel is short and forms a diphthong with the ι that marks this mood.
