25.2:  -μι VERBS WITH THE SUFFIX  –νυ

 

 

δείκ-νυ-μι, δείξ-ω, ἔδειξα, δέδειχ-α, δέδειγ-μαι, ἐ-δείχ-θη-ν = to show

 

If perhaps you cannot conjugate flawlessly, at least you can now recognize the forms of three athematic (-μι) verbs in the present system (present and imperfect of the active and the middle-passive voices), τί-θη-μι, δί-δω-μι, and ἵστημι  To conjugate in the present system the much easier δείκ-νυ-μι all you need to do is add to the present stem the suffix –νυ. 

 

If you consider the citation of  δείκ-νυ-μι, you will see that its other stems are predictable.  It even has a first aorist, ἔδειξα, just like ἔλυσα.

 

Note the following differences of δείκ-νυ-μι with respect to τί-θη-μι and δί-δω-μι: 

 

a) it has no reduplication of the present but adds -νυ to the verb stem 

 

b) in the present system it presents long /short alternance of the vowel, yet not of its stem vowel but the vowel of the suffix  –νυ;

 

c) in the subjunctive and optative it is conjugated as an –ω verb.
