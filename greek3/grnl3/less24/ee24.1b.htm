24.1b:  Athematic verbs: ἵστημι 

The citation of  ἵστημι  is:

 

ἵστημι, στήσω, ἔστησα / ἔστην, ἕστηκα, ἕσταμαι, ἐστάθην

 

Its forms  are not difficult once we understand how they emerged and we recognize that, like other -μι verbs, ἵστημι  is not athematic in all its systems.  In lesson 24, anyway, we focus on the Present system in the Indicative, Subjunctive, and Optative.  See the Chart for these forms.  Yet this verb offers a challenge, not in the forms but in distribution of its transitive and intransitive meanings, which will be explained in 24.2a. 

 

ἵστημι has several features in common with the other two verbs that we are studying in this lesson.  The differences are highlighted in red.

 

·         The Present system is marked by reduplication.  The stem was originally στη / στα.  Initial σ became a rough breathing in Greek, which explains that the Present Indicative went from *σί-στη-μι  to ἵ-στη-μι;

·        the personal endings are the same, but in the 3rd person plural there is contraction of α + α > long  α

·        they alternate long and short  forms of the stem vowel, with a long in the Present Indicative and in the Imperfect singular active, contrasted with a short form of the vowel in the plural active and in the middle / passive Present and Imperfect.  Because α is preceded by a consonant other than ῥ , that is to say, it is an “impure” alpha (remember the first declension?) it lengthens into η, not long α.

·        in the Subjunctive the stem vowel becomes short before another vowel and then contracts with it;

·        in the Optative the stem vowel is short and forms a diphthong with the ι that marks this mood.

 

I will add, in defense of  ἵστημι , that it is not hassled by intruding thematic vowels as are τίθημι  and  δίδωμι (e.g., in the Imperfect ἐτίθεις, ἐδίδουν, etc.)

On the Perfect of ἵστημι

 

Reduplication of the Perfect underwent the same phonetic change as did the reduplication of the Present:  

*σέ-στη-κα  > ἕ-στη-κα.

 

The Perfect active, however, has a surprise in store for us: only the Perfect Indicative active has the ending of the regular perfect, κα.  In other forms the second Perfect plugs the endings right after the short stem of the verb:
