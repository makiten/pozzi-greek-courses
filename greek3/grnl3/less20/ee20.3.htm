20.3  Contract   -όω  verbs

For review of  -έω and   -άω contractions, go to 11.5a, 13.2, and 17.1. 

Again, simplifying the rules at the risk of minor limitations,  we summarize now the contractions of o  with the endings of the Present and Imperfect.  These are the only tenses we need to consider in the Indicative, for, just as in other contract verbs, the vowel is lengthened in the future, aorist, and perfect:  ο  lengthens into  ω.

δηλό-ω > ῶ,  future  δηλώσω, aorist   ἐδήλωσα,   perfect   δεδήλωκα  = to make visible, show

ο + ο  > ου

ο + ε  > ου

ο  + ου  > ου

Do these verbs ever result in anything other than ου ?

YES:

ο + α, η, ω  >  ω

and a funny rule of thumb:

Wherever   ο  meets  ι , whether independent or as part of a diphthong such as ει or οι, it "picks up" that i and forms the diphthong  οι

ο + ι, ει, οι  > οι

If the vowel with which ο combines has  ι subscript,  ο "picks up" the i subscript in the resulting contraction:

ο + ᾳ. ῃ, ῳ  > ῳ

To review accents in contractions, you may go to 11.5a.
