The Middle-Passive Perfect, just like the Active Pefect, was infrequent in the Subjunctive, and instead a periphrasis with the Perfect Participle was used. This participle is an adjective of Type I (1st and 2nd declension) formed by adding  -μένος, -μένη, μένον to the stem shown in the fifth principal part:  πε-παι-δευ-μένος, η, ον.1

Dative of the Agent

Regularly, a personal agent of the passive must be expressed by  ὑπό + the Genitive, and only non-personal agents can be

represented with the dative of means. For review, see 8.2

Examples of the general rule:

 ἐτιμᾶτο ὑπὸ τοῦ δήμου.   He was honored by the people.

  βάλλεται λίθοις.  He is being pelted with rocks.

  τί πέπρακται τοῖς ἄλλοις;    What has been done by the others?

  πολλαὶ θεραπεῖαι εὕρηνται τοῖς ἰατροῖς.   Many cures have been found by doctors.

  τοσαῦτά μοι εἰρέσθω.    Let so much be said by me.
