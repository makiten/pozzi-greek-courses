LESSON 5: Drill on the future of palatal and labial verbs - Vocabulary and tips

ἄρχω, ἄρξω = to rule                                             διώκω, διώξω = to pursue               

γράφω, γράψω = to write, draw                                λείβω, λείψω = to pour1

 

Note 1) The future of this verb, λείψω, is identical to the future of λείπω = to leave (both labial consonants become unvoiced when they combine with σ ), yet in this drill it should be interpreted as the future of λείβω.  Tip:  Both the present and the future may be interpreted as “I do X” or “I am doing X (the progressive English form)”, “I will do X” and “I will be doing X.”  This drill gives sometimes the English progressive forms.  That should be disregarded, since these tenses do not show the  contrast that the grammarians call “aspect.”
