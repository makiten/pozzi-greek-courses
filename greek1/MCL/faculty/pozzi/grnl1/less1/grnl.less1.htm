Suggested strategy: 

Read 1.1 and 1.2a.  Listen to the soundfiles.  Make sure that you understand the examples given before you do the Drill on Nominative and Accusative. 

Do first the exercise for Lesson 1, and then the two drills on Nominative and Accusative.

At this point you may check pages 1-2 of the Summary in 1.4.  If you are happy now, : )  go to 1.2b (read and listen) to get started on the verb.  Again, you may check page 3 of the Summary in 1.4. 

Play with the two drills on the Present Indicative.  (I add the personal pronouns, so you will get used to them)

Read and listen to 1.3.  Now you are ready for the translation and composition exercises.  From the beginning, please remember to mark the functions before you translate.  Composition may be a challenge...  If it seems too difficult, compare the English text with the key and then redo it on your own.

Vocabularies, in the beginning of the course, are given in a simple form.  Try to remember the underscored words, but please understand that memorizing vocabulary is not the focus of this course... You will learn vocabulary more and more easily as you read and practice.
