Review 0.7b:  Parts of speech and their functions

These are the principal functions of nouns, adjectives, and adverbs in a simple clause.

A NOUN may serve primarily  as

SUBJECT

APPOSITIVE

PREDICATE NOMINATIVE

DIRECT OBJECT

INDIRECT OBJECT

Pindar, the poet, wrote songs.

Pindar, the poet, wrote songs.

Pindar was a great poet.

Pindar, the poet, wrote songs.

Pindar, the poet, wrote songs for nobles.

                        

AN ADJECTIVE may modify or depend upon a noun in any of the functions mentioned above:

 

Pindar, a great poet, composed fine songs.

 Pindar, the poet, composed songs for many nobles.

 

AN ADVERB may modify

a VERB

an ADJECTIVE

another ADVERB

 

Historians always admired Herodotus.

Herodotus' works are always interesting.

Herodotus is almost always read in history courses.
