REVIEW 0.4.1: Basic phonetics of vowels  

To avoid hiatus:

    Final vowels are often elided (dropped) if the following word begins with a vowel:  

    ἆρα  εἶ  >  ἀρ’  εἶ

   When, (especially) in conjugation or declension,  an ending beginning with a vowel is applied to a stem ending in a vowel, the two vowels contract.  Simplified rules encontered in this course:

    ε + ε  >   ει

       ε + ο  >  ου     

      ε  +  a long sound  >  that long sound
