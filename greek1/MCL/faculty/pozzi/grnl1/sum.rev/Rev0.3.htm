REVIEW 0.3: Diacritics: breathings and accents

Words beginning with a vowel or a diphthong need a mark called a BREATHING.

The rough breathing indicates that the vowel is preceeded by an aspiration (as English h)

   ἁ  ἑ  ἡ  ἱ  ὁ  ὑ  ὡ  αὑ  εὑ  εἱ, etc.  

(Breathings or accents are marked over the second vowel of a diphthong)

The smooth breathing indicates that there is no aspiration.

     ἀ  ἐ  ἠ  ἰ  ὀ  ὐ  ὠ  αὐ  εὐ  εἰ, etc.  

Accents are of three types:   

   ά  έ  ή  ί  ό  ύ  ώ  αύ  εύ  εί,    etc.  acute    

   ὰ  ὲ  ὴ  ὶ  ὸ  ὺ  ὼ  εὶ,    etc. grave (replaces the acute accent if another word follows).

   ᾶ  ῆ  ῖ  ῦ  ῶ  αῦ,  εῦ,  εῖ, etc. circumflex (may fall only on a long vowel or a diphthong)
