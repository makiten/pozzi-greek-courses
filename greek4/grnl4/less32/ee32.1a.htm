32.1a   εἶμι

FORMS OF εἶμι

 

Languages tend toward simplification and predictability.  In Greek as in other languages, verbs denoting actions and conditions basic to human life are archaic and resilient to change, therefore irregular. εἶμι has several redeeming traits: it has only a present system (on which read further), on the basis of a vowel stem with alternance    εἰ /  ἰ   and no reduplication.  What could make it difficult, you ask?  The answer is: the similarity with forms of other equally frequent -μι   verbs..

 

Breathings and accents are not a luxury here.  They are essential to identify the forms of  εἶμι . Click here for a comparative chart with tips to distinguish similar verbal forms.

MEANINGS OF εἶμι

 

The Indicative is actually a future for ἔρχομαι (ἐλεύσομαι is a late formation).  In contrast, the other moods have present meaning (that is why it has a Subjunctive and an Imperative).
