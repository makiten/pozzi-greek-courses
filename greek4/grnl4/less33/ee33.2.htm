Coordinating conjunctions and particles

1)   Connecting (copulative)

 

καί = and;   τε (enclitic) = and

 

καί may be adverbial.  In this usage it stresses entire clauses or single words. Translate: “also, even.”

 

Ex: καὶ πρίν =  even before; ὀκνῶ καὶ λέγειν =  I fear even to say it   

 

καί   ...  καί, τε  ...  καί  = both... and;

 

οὐδέ (μηδέ)  = and not, nor;

 

Ex: οὐδεμία ἐλπὶς ἦν τιμωρίας, οὐδὲ ἄλλη σωτηρία ἐφαίνετο =   There was no hope of relief, and no other means of safety was apparent.  

     

 οὔτε  ...   οὔτε   (μήτε  ...  μήτε)   =  neither... nor   

 

Ex: οὐκ ἔπειθεν οὔτε τοὺς στρατηγοὺς οὔτε τοὺς στρατιώτας = He persuaded neither the generals nor the soldiers.

 

2)    Expressing contrast (adversative)

 

ἀλλά, the particle δέ  (postpositive, sometimes preceded by μέν)  = but;

μέντοι  (postpositive)  = however  (it may also represent  “in fact”;

 

Ex: ἀφίεμέν σε, ἐπὶ τούτῳ μέντοι...  we let you go, on this condition however  

 

καίτοι   = and yet

 

Ex: ...καίτοι οὐδὲν ὅ τι οὐκ ἀληθὲς εἴρηκα  ...and yet there is nothing untrue in what I have said .

 

3)  Disjunctive

 

ἤ    = or.  It means “than” after comparatives or after the meaning “other,” and the like

 

Ex: οὔκ ἐστιν οὐδὲν κρεῖσσον ἢ φίλος σαφής  There is nothing better than a”clear” (sure) friend.

    ...ἄλλα ἢ τὰ γενόμενα   things different than / from what occurred

 

ἤ  ... ἤ ,  εἴτε  ... εἴτε    =  either... or

 

Ex: ...εἴτ’ οὖν θανόντος εἴτε και ζῶντος πέρι λέγω   ... whether I speak of a dead man or one even alive Ae.Agam 843

 

.4) Inferential

 

ἄρα  = then, accordingly (not to be confused with  ἆρα introducing a question) marks a logical consequence, or is used to recapitulate

 

Ex: (If a and b, then c)  εἰ εἰσὶ βωμοί, εἰσὶ καἰ θεοί· ἀλλὰ μὴν εἰσὶ βωμοί·  εἰσὶν ἄρα καὶ θεοί.   If altars exist, gods exist too.  But indeed altars exist.  Therefore gods exist too.

 

οὖν  = therefore, indicates an objective, factual consequence from what precedes.  Often it is added to retake the thread of the text after a parenthesis or a digression.

 

Ex:  καὶ συγκατέκτανον γάρ, οὐκ ἀρνήσομαι, / καὶ πάντ’ ἐβούλευσ’ ὧν σὺ νῦν τίνεις δίκας· καὶ συνθανεῖν οὖν δεῖ με σοὶ καὶ τῇδ' ὁμοῦ. For I shared in the murder, I will not deny it,  and also schemed the whole plot, for which you are now paying the penalty; and so I ought to die together with you and her.   

 

4) Causal

γάρ  (postpositive) = explaining the reason of what precedes (= “for,” seldom used in conversational English)

 

Ex: φὴς τάδ’ οὖν; ἃ μὴ φρονῶ φὰρ οὐ φιλῶ λέγειν.  Do you then consent to this? //No,// for I am not wont to utter words I do not mean.
