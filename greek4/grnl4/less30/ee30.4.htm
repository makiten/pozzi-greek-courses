30.4:  Prepositional phrases with the dative

 

To complete this selective review of prepositions and their cases in classical Greek we list, 1)  prepositions which can only take the dative, and  2)  prepositions that may accompany three cases: accusative, genitive, or dative. 

 

Remembering the three basic uses of the dative (in lesson 6) may be helpful, as long as we do not press too hard the demand to explain all prepositional phrases by identifying in them those usages.  There are many gray areas, especially in metaphoric uses of the prepositions. 

 

Dative only

 

1)  ἐν, σύν occur with the dative only.

 

ἐν + DATIVE

·         (locative) for space: ἐν τῇ ὕλῃ in the forest; ἐν ὑμῖν  among you, before you (in your presence); for time: ἐν σπονδαῖς  during a truce. 

·        instrumental  ἐν τούτῳ δηλῶσαι to make clear by this

 

σύν + DATIVE (instrumental)

·        It is used (1) in old formulas, as σὺν τοῖς θεοῖς with the help of the gods

·        Manner: σὺν γέλωτι ἦλθον they went(with laughter =)  laughing

 

2) The following prepositions may take the accusative, genitive, and dative:  ἀμφί, ἐπί , μετά, παρά, πρός, υπό

 

ἀμφί + DATIVE  is rare

 

 

ἐπί + DATIVE

 

·        upon, over (especially with verbs meaning to rule): ἐπὶ τῇ τραπέζῃ    ἄρχων ἐπὶ τούτοις ἦν  there was a commander over them

·        upon or against:  ἡ ἐπὶ τᾦ Μήδῳ συμμαχία the alliance against the Medes

·        in addition to:  τὸ ἐπὶ τούτῳ ἀπόκριναι  

 

παρά + DATIVE

 

  • Place, denoting rest by the side of any person or thing, answering the question where? οἱ παρ ἐμοί those of my household; οὐ παρὰ μητρὶ σιτοῦνται οἱ παῖδες, ἀλλὰ παρὰ τῷ διδασκάλῳ  the boys do not eat with their mothers, but with their teachers

 

περί + DATIVE

·        (not common) Place, around (e.i .of dress)  στρεπτοὶ περὶ τοῖς τραχήλοις collars around their necks  

 

πρός + DATIVE

 

·        Place, denoting proximity (generally, in prose, of towns or buildings, not of persons): πρὸς τῇ πόλει τὴν μάχην ποιεῖσθαι   to fight near the city;  αἱ πρὸς τῇ βάσει γωνίαι the angles at the base

 

ὑπό + DATIVE

·        Place, under: ἡ γῆ ἡ ὑπὸ τῷ κόσμῳ κειμένη  the earth under the skies

·        metaphoric, under the influence or authority of,   ἔχειν ὑφ ἑαυτῷ have under one, at ones command
