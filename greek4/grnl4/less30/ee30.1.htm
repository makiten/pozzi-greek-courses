30.1   φημί

       FORMS OF φημί

φημί, to say, is frequent in texts of practically all periods.  You are familiar with a few forms of its present and imperfect.  Here we complete the study of the present system (the future  and the aorist  are regular).

 

With just a glance at the conjugation of φημί  you will recognize that this is an athematic verb with alternance of long / short stem vowel, yet, in contrast to τίθημι, δίδωμι, ἵστημι and ἵημι, it has no reduplication in the present.  

 

The iterative φάσκω, to say / explain, is a handy substitute, even when the forms of φημί  are not unusual:

SYNTAX OF φημί

You have found it in readings accompanying directly reported speech.  It tends to be placed after the quoted discourse has started, often preceding its subject:

 

οὐκοῦν, ἔφη ὁ Σωκράτης, καὶ τῷ γείτονι βούλῃ σὺ ἀρέσκειν, ἵνα σοι καὶ πῦρ ἐναύῃ, ὅταν τούτου δέῃ;

 

Do you want to please your neighbor so that he may kindle a fire for you, whenever you need it?

 

The imperfect translates into English as a simple past:  I said, X said.  Verbs denoting mental and communicative actions tend to be used in the imperfect, especially if the content of thought or discourse is made explicit (that makes the report progressive.).

 

προσελθοῦσα οὖν μοι ἐγγὺς ἡ ἄνθρωπος ευφίλητε, ἔφη, μηδεμιᾷ πολυπραγμοσύνῃ προσεληλυθέναι με νόμιζε πρὸς σέ.

 

Thus the woman, approaching me closely, said,  Dear Sir, do not think that I am coming to you with any meddlesome purpose.

In lesson 12.4a, where indirect statements with the structure Accusative + Infinitive was presented, you learned that this is the only type of indirect statement that φημί  governs.  When used with οὐ, the negation normally applies to the verb in the indirect statement, as in:

 

τὰ μὲν οὖν ὀνόματα οὐκ ἔφη αὐτῶν ἐρεῖν καθ' ἕκαστον

He declined to give their names individually (one by one)

 

ἐὰν δ οὐ φάσκῃ, ἔρεσθε αὐτὸν δι ὅ τι φησὶν Ἀθηναῖος ποιηθῆναι.

And if he will not tell, ask him on what grounds  he  says that he was made an Athenian.

The deponents δύναμαι = to be able, ἐπίσταμαι = to understand, in the present,  and ἐπριάμην = bought (athematic middle aorist that ἵστημι lacks) have a predictable conjugation with these exceptions:

 

Imperfect 2nd sg  ἐδύνω, ἠπίστω, ἐπρίω

Subjunctive and Optative with recessive accent:  δύνωμαι, δύναιτο
