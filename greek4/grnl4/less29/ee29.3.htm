29.3:  Prepositional phrases with the genitive

 

Again, we need to start by listing, 1)  prepositions which can only take the genitive, and  2) those that may take accusative or genitive.  The prepositions that may accompany three cases: accusative, genitive, or dative, will be addressed in lesson 30.

 

As stated with respect to the accusative, remembering the basic uses of the genitive may be helpful, as long as we do not press too hard the demand to explain all prepositional phrases by identifying in them those usages.  There are many gray areas, especially in metaphoric uses of the prepositions. 

 

Genitive only

1)  ἀντί, ἀπό, ἐκ   occur with the genitive only.  When they are spatial or temporal, “direction from, provenance, or origin” are often clear.

 

ἀντί +  GENITIVE

·        opposite to:  ἀντὶ μαρτύρων, in the presence of witnesses; instead of: τὸν πόλεμον ἀντ' εἰρήνης μεταλαμβάνειν  to exchange peace for war;

 

ἀπό +  GENITIVE

·        Local and temporal:  from, off, away from, ἡ μάχη ἦν ἀφ’ ἵππων;  ἀφ’ ἑσπέρας after evening began (after sundown); ἀπὸ θεῶν ἀρχόμενοι  beginning with the gods;

·        Cause (remote):  ταῦτα οὐκ ἀπὸ τύχης ἐγίγνετο, ἀλλ’ ἀπὸ παρασκευῆς τῆς ἐμῆς   this happened not from chance but by reason of the preparations I made;

 

ἐκ +  GENITIVE

·        Local and temporal:  ἐκ χρυσῶν φιαλῶν πίνειν   to drink from golden bowls;  ἐκ παίδων  from boyhood

·        Other relations.  Immediate succession or transition: ἐκ πτωχῶν πλούσιοι γίγνονται   from beggars they become rich; ἐκ πολέμου ποιούμενος εἰρήνην  making peace after (a state of) war; origin: immediate origin (ἀπό  is used of remote origin): ἀγαθοὶ καὶ ἐξ ἀγαθῶν    noble and of noble breed  

 

 

πρό +  GENITIVE

·        πρό  before. Cf. ἀντί , which is narrower in meaning.

·        Local and temporal:  πρὸ τῶν ἀμαξῶν  in front of the wagons;  πρὸ τῆς μἀχης  before the battle

·        Other relations.  Defence or care (cf. ὑπέρ): διακινδυνεύειν πρὸ βασιλέως    to incur danger in defence of (prop. in front of) the king; preference (cf. ἀντί):   οἱ ἐπαινοῦντες πρὸ δικαιοσύνης ἀδικίαν  thοse who praise  injustice in preference to justice

 

2) The following prepositions may take the accusative and genitive:  ἀμφί, διά, κατά, μετά, ὑπέρ

 

ἀμφί +  GENITIVE  (rare in prose)  

·        about (cf  περί + gen)  ἀμφὶ σῆς λέγω παιδός I speak about your child  

 

διά +  GENITIVE

·        Local: through, originally through and out of: διὰ θαλάσσης across the sea; temporal: of uninterrupted duration, διὰ νυκτός  through the night  

·        Separation by cleavage, especially as preverb, as in  διαγιγνώσκω, distinguish, discern, cf. diagnosis.  Figurative:  αὐτοῖς διὰ φιλίας ἰέναι to enter into friendship with them

 

κατά +  GENITIVE

·        Local. Down, opposed to  ἀνά  Down from: κατὰ  τοῦ τείχους  down from the wall.  With the genitive, the motion is perpendicular (down from); with the accusative, horizontal (down along).

 

·        Other relations.   Against, as κατ’ ἐμαυτοῦ ἐρεῖν  to speak against myself;  ὅπερ εἴρηται καθόλου κατὰ πασῶν τῶν πολιτειῶν  what is said in general against all the constitutions

 

 

μετά +  GENITIVE

Usually of persons and abstract nouns:  μετ’ αὐτοῦ with him  

·        Local: among, together with, as καθήμενος μετὰ τῶν ἄλλων  sitting among the rest; μετ’ οὐδενὸς ἀνδρῶν ναίνειν  among no men

 

ὑπέρ +  GENITIVE

·        Local. Οver, above: πόλις ὑπὲρ λιμένος κεῖται the city lies above the harbor; from over: ὑπὲρ τῶν ἄκρων κατέβαινον  they came down over the heights

·        Other relations.   In defence of, on behalf of:  μαχόμενος ὑπὲρ ὑμῶν  fighting for you (standing over to protect); in place of, in the name of: ἐγὼ λέξω καὶ ὑπὲρ σοῦ καὶ ὑπὲρ ἡμῶν  I will speak both for you and for ourselves 

 

3) Prepositions used with three cases, accusative, genitive, and dative: ἐπί, παρά, περί, πρός, ὑπό

 

ἐπί +  GENITIVE

·        Local.  Upon, on, on the surface of; opposed to ὑπό  under, and to ὑπὲρ  when ὑπὲρ   means above the surface of: οὔτ’ ἐπὶ γῆς οὔθ’ ὑπὸ γῆς   neither upon the earth nor under the earth  

·        Temporal, usually with personal gen.  Ιn the time of: ἐπὶ τῶν προγόνων   in the time of our ancestors  

 

παρά +  GENITIVE

Αlongside, by, near. Except with the accusative, παρά   is commonly used of persons and personified things.

·        Local: οἱ αὐτομολοῦντες παρὰ βασιλέως  the deserters from the king;  ἀπαγγέλλειν τι παρά τινος  to bring an announcement from someone

·        Other relations.   Source: with verbs of receiving, taking, asking, learning, sending, etc.:  παρὰ σοῦ ἐμάθομεν   we learned from you

 

περί +  GENITIVE

·        Local: around (on all sides), about;  περί  is wider than ἀμφί:  οὐ περίρρυτος οὖσα ὥσπερ νῆσος... ἀμφιθάλλατος γάρ ἐστι  (Attica) is  not, like an island, surrounded by the sea . . . for it has the sea on two sides. 

·        Other relations.  About, concerning: περὶ πατρίδος μαχούμενοι  fighting for their country;  superiority περὶ παντὸς ποιεῖσθαι  to regard  as (more than everything) all-important 

 

πρός +  GENITIVE

·        Descent:  πρὸς πατρός  on the father's side;  point of view of a person: πρὸς ἀνθρώπων αἰσχρός  base in the eyes of men; by: πρὸς θεῶν  in the name of the gods

 

 ὑπό +  GENITIVE

·        Local (rare in prose)  τὰ ὑπὸ γῆς ἅπαντα   all things under the earth

·        Direct agent (with passives and with verbs having a passive force).  εὖ ἀκούειν ὑπὸ ἀνθρώπων   to be well spoken of by men   External cause: ἀπώλετο ὑπὸ λιμοῦ    perished of hunger;  Internal cause: ὑπὸ τῶν μεγίστων νικηθέντες, τιμῆς καὶ δέους καὶ ὠφελίας   constrained by the strongest motives, honour and fear and profit .
