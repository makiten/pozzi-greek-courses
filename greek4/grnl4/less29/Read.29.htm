[1] Ἀνάχαρσις, εως, ὁ

[2] Ionic genitive of the name of the presocratic philosopher Thales (Θαλῆς)

[3] Plutarch reports the story using an anonymous 3rd person plural.

[4] With this accent, this particle means “therefore”; with the accent on the first part of the compound, used to introduce a question, it means “Isn’t it true that...?”

[5]  Ιn the first aorist of verbs with three syllables (that have a middle voice), three forms are identical except for the accent :   ποίησαι = 2nd sg imperative middle; ποιῆσαι = active aorist infinitive; ποιήσαι = 3rd sg active aorist optative.

[6]  Verbs with the preverb κατά  meaning “against” take a complement in the genitive.

[7]  This particle should not be translated; its function is often, as here, that of pointing to the addressee (it comes from an earlier σοι).  If we translated it, we should say something like a colloquial “I’m telling you,”  “see?” or the like.
