12.2:  αὐτός in predicative and attributive positions

 

Hopefully, you now recognize immediately in its context the functions of  αὐτός as a personal pronoun: emphatic in the Nominative, equivalent to "him, her, it, them" in the other ("oblique") cases.  But  αὐτός has two other applications that need to be distinguished carefully.  It would be fair to say that in both it is used rather as an adjective than as a pronoun, because they are contrasted by the position αὐτός  takes with respect to the noun to which it refers, or, to put it differently, with respect to the article:

 

In the attributive position (1st or 2nd) or used by itself with an article it means "the same."

 

   ὁ αὐτὸς λόγος

or

   ὁ λόγος ὁ αὐτὸς

  the same story

   ἡ αὐτὴ ἁμαρτία

or

  ἡ ἁμαρτία ἡ αὐτὴ  

  the same error (classical Greek)

  the same sin (koinê Greek)

  τὸ αὐτὸ ὄνομα

or

  τὸ ὄνομα τὸ αὐτὸ

  the same name

    used as pronouns:   

   ὁ αὐτός, ἡ αὐτή, τὸ αὐτό

   the same man, the same woman, the same thing

 

Tip: If you remember that the demonstrative  οὗτος, αὕτη, τοῦτο has the rough breathing in the masculine and feminine Nominative of sg and pl, you will never confuse it with  αὐτός, αὐτή, αυτό.  

 

In the predicative position it is emphatic, not just in the nominative but in the other cases as well.  It may be placed before or after the noun.

 

   αὐτὸς ὁ ποιμήν

or

  ὁ ποιμήν αὐτός 

  the shepherd himself

  ᾿Ιωκάστη αὐτή

or

  αὐτὴ ᾿Ιωκάστη   

  Jocasta herself

   τὸ αἴνιγμα αὐτό

or

  αὐτὸ τὸ αἴνιγμα

  the riddle itself

    αὐτὸ τοῦτο

   this / that itself, this / that very thing

 

It may be used with subjects of the second and third persons as well:

 

   ἐγὼ αὐτός

   I myself

   σὺ αὐτός

  you (sg) yourself   

   ἡμεῖς αὐτοί

  we ourselves
   ὑμεῖς αὐτοί   you (pl) yourselves

 

  

EXAMPLES

 

a)   οὐδὲν ἀεὶ τὸ αὐτὸ μένει.

           S          adv     pred nom         V  

Nothing remains always the same. 

 

Note: Remember that to be is by no means the only linking verb either in Greek or in English.  "Remain" (= be permanently) takes a predicate nominative in this example.  

 

b) δὶς οὐκ ἔχομεν ἐμβαίνειν εἰς τὸν αὐτὸν ποταμόν.

     adv                        V             compl infin        ....................   εἰς   +   acc  ....................

We cannot step twice into the same river.  (Note that ἔχω  + infinitive = to be able to...)

This sentence is freely adapted from Heracleitus, a presocratic philosopher.  It is his main tenet, that everything is in a constant flux.

 

c)  αὕτη δέ εστιν ἡ φωνὴ τοῦ Κυρίου αὐτοῦ.

             S                     V              P N                   ............   gen  ................

This is the voice of the Lord himself.

 

d) αὐτὸς ἔφη.

            S              V     

 He himself said (it). =  The Master (Pythagoras) said it.

The members of Pythagorean sects referred to the philosopher Pythagoras as αὐτός  in recognition of his religious authority. 

Learn  ἔφη = he/she said.  It is the 3rd person sg of a highly irregular verb. Very few forms of this verb were used (it is a defective verb), but these were quite frequent.

e)  καὶ αὐτὸς πορεύσομαι μεθ’ ὑμῶν.

                      w/S                     V                      μετά +  gen 

And I myself will walk with you (pl).
