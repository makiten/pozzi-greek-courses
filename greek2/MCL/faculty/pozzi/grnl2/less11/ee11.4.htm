11.4: Stems ending in dental, labial, and palatal consonants

 

Remember the results of the combination of these consonants with the  σ of the future tense of verbs?  In 3.1 we saw how dental consonants drop before σ, and  in 5.2 how labial and palatal consonants before  σ first become unvoiced and then are spelled as double consonants.  The same phonetic changes occur when noun stems ending in those consonants combine with   σ  in the 3rd declension.  They will occur in some nominatives (those marked by  σ) and in all plural datives.  The chart in 11.3 summarizes once again those combinations, with examples from the 3rd declension.  Here is the complete declension of one noun of each consonantal variety.  Notice that stems ending in ντ will be studied in another lesson: they cannot be included with other dental stems!  

Two practical observations: a) in nouns of the 3rd declension that have  σ  in the Nominative sg, the phonetic changes that take place in the Nom sg will also occur in the Dative pl.; b) neuter nouns do not mark the Nom sg with either lengthening of the vowel or  σ.
