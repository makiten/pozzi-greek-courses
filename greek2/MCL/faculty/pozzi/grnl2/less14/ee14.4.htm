14.4  Adverbial clauses: an introduction

 

An adverbial clause usually modifies a conjugated verb or another verbal form.  There are several kinds of adverbial clauses, and we will not exhaust their study this semester.  Here we start by listing the types of adverbial clauses that we have already encountered.  It is easy to recognize their adverbial function if one considers that the kinds of questions they answer, e.g. "when?"  "why?"  "on what condition?"  relate to circumstances of the verbal action.  Let us pair them with the simple adverbs they parallel.

 


 

ὁ φιλόσοφος κατέπεσε  

 

 

 

 

The philosopher fell.  [when?]... when he was looking up at the sky. 

 

τὰ γένη τῶν ἀνθρώπων φθείρεται.      

  

 

 

 

The generations of men perish  [how?]...just as the generations of leaves perish . 

 

σὲ κολάσω.      

 

 

 

 

 

I will punish you [why?] because I consider you evil.

 

τοῖς πλουσίοις οὐ φθονῶ.     [upon what condition?]

 

 

 

I do not envy the rich [upon what condition?]  if they are not happy.
