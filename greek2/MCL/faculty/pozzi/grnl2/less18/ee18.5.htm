18.5  Uses of the dependent Subjunctive: Subjunctive + ἄν, 3

 

IN RELATIVE CLAUSES

 

 

                                        main verb in any tense

I  

  indicative

 (any tense)

Ia ( ὃς τοῦτο ποιεῖ) [ ἐγκωμιάζεται.]

Ib  [τὸν ἄνδρα (  ὃς τοῦτο ποιήσει) ἐγκωμιάσω ]

He who does this is praised.

 

I will praise the man who will do this.

II

   ἄν + the Subjunctive 3

main verb in the present:            generalizing meaning

IIa  ( ὅστις ἂν τοῦτο ποιῇ ) [ἐγκωμιάζεται.]

 Whoever does this is (always) praised.

main verb in the future:         future meaning

Ib  [ τὸν ἄνδρα ... 

( ὃς ἂν τοῦτο ποιήσῃ)         ...     ἐγκωμιάσω ]

I will praise the man who will do this.

 

EXAMPLES

IIa  ὃς ἂν  θεοῖς ἐπιπείθηται, πάντες αὐτῷ ἀκολουθοῦσιν.   

 Whoever puts faith in the gods, all follow him.

IIb  ἀπόκριναι2  ὅ τι ἂν ἐρωτῶ.3

Answer whatever I ask you.

 

Notes

1: Note that the constructions in Ib and IIb have the same meaning (except for stylistic differences that we will not consider yet)

2: This is the aorist imperative, 2nd sg, of  ἀποκρίνομαι, a deponent verb  

3:  ἐρωτῶ must be interpreted as subjunctive (although in a contracted  -άω verb present indicative and present subjunctive have the same forms) because of the presence of    ἄν,  which is never found with the present indicative.
